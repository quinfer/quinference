The rule at the heart of this dispute. The GAA Football Review Committee's 2025 rule enhancements state that two points are awarded for a ball "cleanly kicked from play or a free over the bar from on or outside the arc, and without touching another player on either team." A sideline kick is not mentioned.
Source: GAA FRC Rule Enhancements Explainer, gaa.ie, January 2025 — Official Guide Part 2, GAA, January 2025

What happened

Derry are leading Monaghan by two points with ten seconds remaining. The ball goes over the sideline near Derry's own goal — Derry touched it last. A sideline kick is awarded to Monaghan. Monaghan take the kick from outside the 40m arc, the ball sails over the bar, and the referee raises two hands. Derry dispute the award of two points. If it stands, the sides are level and extra time follows — which Monaghan win.

LEGAL ANALYSIS

In practice, GAA disciplinary bodies would likely apply the Official Guide strictly. The two-point scoring provision in the FRC Rule Enhancements (gaa.ie, January 2025) confines the award to "play or a free" — a sideline kick falls into neither category. The "45" express exclusion further supports a one-point reading. However, if Derry's designated captain did not formally protest before play restarted, the window for challenge may have closed under the principle of referee finality. That procedural dimension is as legally interesting as the substantive question — and underscores why the 2025 rules require urgent clarification on this precise point.
